I am a PhD candidate in Computer Science at Case Western Reserve University, where I work in the Security, Privacy, and Data Science Laboratory (SPiD Lab) with my advisor Professor Erman Ayday. My research focuses on AI security and forensics, including watermarking and provenance tracking for large language models and AI-generated content; AI governance and policy frameworks for responsible deployment; and trustworthy AI methods to ensure systems are transparent, accountable, and reliable in real-world applications. I am a member of the Case Western Reserve AI Task Force, which promotes equitable and ethical access to AI training and tools across the university. I am completing a research internship at Sandia National Laboratories focusing on AI security.

I graduated from Ohio University with Bachelor of Science degrees in Computer Science and Psychology. As a Choose Ohio First scholar, I presented research on machine learning for network attack detection at the Ohio Statehouse. During my undergraduate studies, I developed a mobile application for foster children to preserve childhood memories, worked on signal processing for nuclear physics experiments at Jefferson Lab, and interned at Cisco and Sherwin-Williams.
