About Me

I'm a PhD candidate at Case Western Reserve University specializing in AI security and trustworthiness. My research focuses on watermarking and forensics for large language models, essentially creating ways to trace and verify AI-generated content.

With a background spanning both Computer Science and Psychology, I bring a unique perspective to understanding how AI systems interact with people and society. I'm passionate about building AI that's not just powerful, but transparent and accountable.

My work addresses critical questions: How do we detect AI-generated content? How do we ensure AI systems are trustworthy across different industries? How do we govern these technologies responsibly?

Selected Publications

Topic-Based Watermarks for Large Language Models

Alexander Nemecek, Yuzhou Jiang, Erman Ayday

TL;DR

This paper proposes a topic-based watermarking scheme for large language models that partitions vocabulary into semantically aligned token subsets rather than random lists. We demonstrate that our method achieves comparable text quality to industry systems like Google's SynthID-Text while providing enhanced robustness against paraphrasing and lexical perturbation attacks, offering a lightweight approach that requires minimal computational overhead and facilitates straightforward adoption in production pipelines.

Watermarking Without Standards Is Not AI Governance

Alexander Nemecek, Yuzhou Jiang, Erman Ayday

TL;DR

This paper argues that current watermarking for AI-generated content lacks the technical robustness and standardization needed for effective governance, often failing basic transformations like paraphrasing. We propose a three-layer framework requiring technical standards, independent audits, and enforceable policies to transform watermarking from symbolic compliance into meaningful accountability.
